Results of Google Forms survey

Please read below to view the results of survey started for the general public regarding their knowledge on Internet of Things, Smart Devices and the vulnerabilities and risks that may occur within their smart home.

Note: 144 participants entered, with a minimum of 107 responses.

The first question on this survey was regarding the age group of each participant. The majority of these users were 18-24 and 25-34.

Almost 20% of the participants were unaware of the Internet of Things or Smart Devices. The participants that did not know were removed from the upcoming questions.

With 144 responses, it was determined at least more than 70% of these participants have got a smart device. That is 100 of the 144.

The next question was regarding why they use smart devices. The majority of these qualitative answers were the following: Ease of use, quality of life. automation and accessibility and listening to music.

Excluding the 21.5% of people with 0 devices, the average of smart devices in one household is 4, with 2 and 3 being the next highest amount.

This chart displays that the majority of the participants do not have full trust in the smart devices that they have installed within their household. This is a worrying statistic, demonstrating that people will use devices that they do not fully trust.

Almost 40% of the participants believe they do not feel secure enough with their smart devices within their smart home.

To mitigate risks and to uphold a strong security within your smart home, network segmentation is needed as it will not have your import devices such as your phones and consoles on the same network as possible in-secure IoT devices such as smart lights. Over 80% of users do not do this.

With the previous data showing that the majority of users do not segment their network, it is worrying to display that almost 50% of the participants are not worried of the vulnerabilities or possible risks of their devices. With 40% aware and worried, is there a lack of information and support to help the general public mitigate risks in their home?

Lastly, the two last questions focused on the participant's knowledge and awareness on what a potential hacker can do and what information they can discover if there are vulnerabilities.

When it came to what hackers could do, most participants answered with that they were unsure or unaware and they they could jam their devices and take sensitive data from them.

Sadly, one of the main answers was that users were unsure of what hackers could access, with another smaller majority saying that personal identifiable information could be used such as bank information, passwords and location of the device and user.
